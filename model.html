Our Model

Combining proven lay therapy with continuous innovation

What is Lay Therapy?

Lay therapy involves training community members without formal mental health degrees to deliver evidence-based therapeutic interventions. This approach has been validated through more than 100 randomized controlled trials showing effectiveness comparable to professional therapy for many common mental health conditions.

Key characteristics of effective lay therapy programs:

  • Community members are trained in specific, structured interventions
  • Regular supervision ensures quality and fidelity
  • Cultural adaptation makes treatments relevant and acceptable
  • Group formats leverage peer support and reduce costs
  • Focus on common conditions like depression and anxiety

The LATERAL Difference

While many organizations implement lay therapy programs, LATERAL is building something unique: a permanent research and innovation platform that benefits the entire global mental health movement.

Permanent Research Platform

Unlike one-off implementations, we're creating lasting infrastructure for continuous learning. Every therapy session contributes to our understanding of what works, for whom, and under what conditions.

Collective Intelligence

Our network learns from every interaction. Insights from one community inform improvements across all communities, creating a virtuous cycle of enhancement.

Cultural Adaptation Core

We don't impose one-size-fits-all solutions. Each community's program is adapted to local culture, language, and values while maintaining therapeutic effectiveness.

Technology-Enhanced Supervision

Digital tools help us provide consistent, high-quality supervision to lay therapists regardless of location, ensuring fidelity while reducing costs.

Our Innovation Engine

LATERAL's feedback loop system creates continuous improvement:

  1. Data Collection: We gather information about therapy outcomes, cultural contexts, and implementation challenges
  2. Analysis: Our research team identifies patterns and opportunities for improvement
  3. Innovation: We develop and test new approaches, tools, and adaptations
  4. Implementation: Successful innovations are rolled out across our network
  5. Evaluation: We measure impact and start the cycle again

This approach allows us to move beyond static programs to create a dynamic, learning system that improves over time.

How We Implement

Community Partnership

We begin by partnering with local organizations and leaders who understand their community's needs and can help ensure cultural fit.

Lay Therapist Selection

Community members are carefully selected based on empathy, communication skills, and community standing rather than formal education.

Intensive Training

Lay therapists receive comprehensive training in evidence-based interventions, adapted for local context and delivered in local languages.

Ongoing Supervision

Regular supervision sessions ensure quality, provide support, and create opportunities for continuous learning and improvement.

Group Therapy Delivery

Therapy is primarily delivered in group formats, leveraging peer support while making services more accessible and cost-effective.

Outcome Measurement

We systematically measure outcomes to ensure effectiveness and identify opportunities for enhancement.
